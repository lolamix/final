About Lola


My name is Lola, and I'm an artist with a diverse and rich background. I was born in the Bronx but spent my childhood in the Dominican Republic, which has greatly influenced my artistic vision and style. From a young age, painting has been an important part of my life. I enjoy experimenting with various mediums, whether it's oil, acrylic, watercolor, or mixed media.

One of my favorite aspects of creating art is incorporating different textures into my paintings. I believe that textures add a unique dimension and depth to my work, making each piece more engaging and tactile. Whether it's through the use of unconventional materials or layered paint techniques, I strive to create pieces that invite viewers to not just see but also feel the emotions conveyed.

A central theme in my art is mental health. I am deeply passionate about exploring and expressing the complexities of the human mind and emotions through my work. Art has always been a therapeutic outlet for me, and I hope to inspire others to find solace and understanding through creative expression. My paintings often reflect personal experiences, struggles, and triumphs, aiming to foster a sense of connection and empathy in those who view them.

Through my art, I aim to create a dialogue about mental health, breaking down stigmas and encouraging others to look into their own mental health.
